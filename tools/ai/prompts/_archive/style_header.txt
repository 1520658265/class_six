STYLE: 16-bit JRPG era pixel art, in the spirit of late-SNES / early-GBA RPGs (Final Fantasy VI, Chrono Trigger, EarthBound) and modern cozy indies like Eastward — readable chunky pixels, clean 1-pixel black or dark-brown outlines, no anti-aliasing, no smooth gradients, only flat colors with subtle dithered shading where needed.

THEME: 1990s rural Chinese village elementary school. Warm, nostalgic, slightly sun-bleached. Color palette is intentionally limited and cohesive across the whole tileset:
  - grass green: muted olive/sage, NOT vivid neon green
  - foliage: deep pine green with a hint of teal
  - dirt / mud / road: warm yellow-brown ochre
  - playground sand: pale sandy tan, slightly grey
  - brick wall: warm terracotta red, weathered, with darker mortar lines
  - roof tile: cool weathered grey with a hint of blue-green
  - water: dusty muted teal, NOT bright cyan
  - wood: warm light tan / aged pine
  - accent for shop sign: faded cobalt blue

OUTPUT FORMAT:
  - Single asset, centered, no UI, no text, no labels, no border, no watermark, no signature, no shadow on the background.
  - Pure magenta #FF00FF flat solid background (used as a chroma-key for alpha).
  - Render so the visible pixel grid corresponds to a 32x32-pixel asset scaled up — i.e. clearly chunky pixels, not a smooth painting.
  - Strict top-down view unless the asset is a building (see per-asset notes).
